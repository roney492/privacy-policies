# Fish World — Privacy Notice

_Last updated: May 26, 2026_

This notice explains, in plain English, what Fish World does and does not
do with information. Fish World is a game for kids ages 2–8, so this
notice is written for the parent or guardian who installs it.

If you only read one section, read this one: **Fish World runs entirely
on your child's device. We do not have servers that store your child's
data. We do not show ads. We do not sell anything inside the game.**

---

## 1. Who we are

Fish World is created and published by **Roney Dsilva**, an independent
solo developer. There is no company — Fish World is a one-person project.
Roney is the person responsible for your child's data under this notice
(the "data controller"). You can get in touch any time at
**roney4972@gmail.com**.

---

## 2. What we collect

We collect the **minimum** needed to make the game work:

- **Profile name** — a short text label (e.g. "Sam") that you, the
  parent, type in when you create a profile for your child. This is
  not verified. We recommend using a first name or nickname, not a
  full legal name.
- **Local game progress** — which levels your child has completed,
  stickers earned, sound on/off, etc. Stored on the device only.
- **Anonymous crash logs** — if the app crashes, Android sends a stack
  trace to Google Play Console so we can fix the bug. These logs are
  technical and do not contain any identifier we set. You can disable
  this in Android Settings (search for "Usage & diagnostics").

That is the entire list.

---

## 3. What we do NOT collect

We want to be explicit. Fish World does **not** collect or transmit any
of the following:

- Android Advertising ID (AAID)
- Device serial number, IMEI, MAC address, SIM serial
- Precise or coarse location
- Contacts, photos, microphone audio, camera images
- Biometric data (fingerprint, face geometry, voice prints)
- Account identifiers (Google account, email address)
- IP address (the app never makes a network request in v1)
- Browsing history, app usage outside Fish World

We also do not show ads, do not include any third-party analytics SDK,
and do not include any social media SDK.

---

## 4. Where data is stored

- **On the device.** All profile and progress data lives in the app's
  private sandboxed storage on your child's phone or tablet. Only
  Fish World can read it.
- **Optionally, in your Google Drive.** If you have Android Auto Backup
  turned on for your device (it's on by default in Android), Android
  may copy app data to **your own Google Drive backup** so it can be
  restored if you switch phones. This backup belongs to you, not us.
  We never see it. You control it from Settings → Google → Backup.
  You can delete it any time.
- **Nowhere else.** We do not operate servers that store your data.

---

## 5. Retention policy

### What we retain

Fish World is an offline, on-device app. The only data that exists is
on the child's device.

| Item | Purpose | Business need | Retention period |
|---|---|---|---|
| Profile name | Lets the child pick "their" profile. | Multiple kids may share one tablet. | Until the parent deletes the profile in-app, or uninstalls the app. |
| Game progress | Lets the child resume where they left off. | Core gameplay function. | Same as above. |
| Crash diagnostics | Lets us fix bugs. Stack traces only; no identifier we set. | Bug fixing. | Held by Google Play's crash pipeline per Google's retention; we keep no separate copy. |

### Why

We retain on-device data only as long as it serves the child's
gameplay experience. The retention period is anchored to your
action: when you delete the profile or uninstall the app, the data
goes away.

### How it is deleted

- Tap **Delete profile** in-app: immediate deletion from the device.
- Uninstall the app and answer "No" to the "Keep app data?" prompt:
  Android deletes everything.
- Delete the Auto Backup in your Google Drive: the off-device copy is
  gone.

We do not have a server-side delete button because we do not have a
server.

---

## 6. Third parties

For v1 of Fish World, there are no third parties. Specifically:

- No ad network.
- No analytics SDK.
- No A/B testing SDK.
- No social login.
- No push notification service.

The only third party in the picture is **Google**, because the app is
distributed through Google Play and Google's crash-reporting pipeline
runs at the OS level. We are not in control of Google's data practices;
see Google's privacy policy at https://policies.google.com/privacy.

If we ever add a third-party SDK we will update this notice **before**
shipping the update, and we will only consider SDKs that appear on
Google Play's **Families Self-Certified Ads SDKs** list and are
compliant with COPPA, GDPR-K, and the UK Children's Code.

---

## 7. Parental rights

As the parent or guardian, you can:

- **Review** the data we have about your child: open Fish World, tap
  **Settings → Profiles**. Everything we have is shown there.
- **Delete** the data: tap **Delete profile** in Settings, or
  uninstall the app.
- **Export** the data: tap **Settings → Export profile** to write a
  JSON file to your device's Downloads folder. You can open it in any
  text editor.
- **Refuse further collection**: since we don't collect anything
  beyond what's described above, there's nothing additional to refuse.
  If we ever add optional collection we will ask for opt-in consent
  with a parental gate.
- **Contact us**: email roney4972@gmail.com. We respond within
  7 business days.

## 8. Legal summaries

### COPPA (United States, under 13)

We comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act and its
2025 amendments:

- We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under
  13 beyond what is described in section 2.
- Our retention policy is in section 5 of this notice (per the amended
  rule, retention disclosures must appear in the privacy notice
  itself).
- We do not condition a child's participation in any activity on the
  child disclosing more information than is reasonably necessary.
- If you believe your child has provided us information and you would
  like it deleted, email roney4972@gmail.com.

### GDPR-K (EU/EEA, under 16 — varies by country, down to 13)

We rely on the legal basis of **legitimate interest** in providing a
working game, scoped to the minimal on-device data described above. We
do not transfer your child's data out of the device. You have rights
of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and
objection — all of which are exercised through the in-app controls in
section 7 because the data lives on your device.

### CCPA / CPRA (California, under 16)

We do not sell or share personal information of any user, including
minors. The CCPA's "do not sell" requirement is effectively a no-op for
us because we do not sell. California residents may email
roney4972@gmail.com to exercise rights of access and deletion.

### UK Children's Code (Age Appropriate Design Code)

Fish World is designed against the 15 standards of the UK Children's
Code:

- **Best interests of the child** drives design decisions.
- **Data minimisation**: see section 2 — minimum necessary only.
- **No nudge techniques** to extend session time.
- **No geolocation**, no profiling, no targeted advertising.
- **Default settings are high privacy** out of the box.
- **Transparency**: this notice is written for parents in plain English.

### Google Play Age Signals API

Where required by Google Play, Fish World uses the Age Signals API to
receive an age-category signal at install time (no exact age, no
identifier). Fish World's behavior is identical for all users because
the app is designed for under-13 use from the outset.
